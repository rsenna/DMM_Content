Note that the audio examples contained herein are simply that; examples. They have not been mastered and are not to state that "This is how it should be done" but rather "This is how its currently done". They are simply included to show how the techniques described in the manual make a difference to a musical production. It is up to you to show us how it is done in the future.

Perhaps more importantly, Electronic Dance Music has become a truly disparate genre. It has fragmented with a diversity no-one could have imagined twenty years ago but it has also resulted in countless immaterial arguments over what constitutes specific genres. This only serves to draw attention away from the goal of actually creating music and is not the focus of the Manual or these examples. If you wish to argue semantics there are many public forums available on the world wide web to waste your time on.

Truly successful producers and artists are those that have looked beyond themselves and their egos and focused on the music, ideas, production and techniques employed in any and all genres. Without this attitude, dance music would not have developed nor survived with the success it has today. Unquestionably, one of the biggest paths to a producers success is to open your mind as well as your ears.
