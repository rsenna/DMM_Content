Chapter 22 - Composing and Designing Leads

Except for only a few genres, electronic dance music does not typically employ melodies and instead will rely on a simple motif. Also known as a motive, hook or theme, this is a rhythmical idea that is repeated throughout the music. Its aim is to provide listeners with a reference point for the track. The opening bars of Eric Prydz Pjanoo, the repeating chord patterns in Major Lazer Lean on or Avicci Levels, Calvin Harris use of rhythmical chords in Lets Go, and the growing synth rhythm in DeadMau5 Faxing Berlin are all great examples of motifs. As soon as you hear them, you can immediately identify with the track, and if they are composed well, they can be compelling tools.

Composing Leads

Avicci "Levels" Played on a Piano

Composed in C# Minor (relative of E Major), Avicci's "Levels" employs the familiar compositional motif. Here, note how the first passage of the motif "asks" a question that is responded in the second part with a change in pitch at the end.

Within electronic dance music, motifs such as this are the preferred approach over longer more exotic melodies. When composing these styles of motifs it is common practice to retain the same rhythm through both parts and only employ small changes in the second to provide the "answer".
