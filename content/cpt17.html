Chapter 17 - Kicks & Percussion

The kick drum has become one of the most important timbre in many genres of electronic dance music. Although originally the TR909 was responsible for many kick drums in earlier years, more recently the kick has become larger than life and many producers will spend hours or even days working to produce their own kick drum sound.

The Individual Samples

Samples

The above example consists of the three kicks chosen to layer in order to produce a kick. The first sample has been chosen for its transient "clicky" nature, whilst the second chosen because it will provide a good mid-range body and the third can be used to provide the low end sub.

The Kick Layered

The layered Kick Drum

Each kick was placed on its own audio channel, aligned, the centre kick was phase inverted and then audio editing was applied to separate the kicks into three distinct components. These were mixed together at the desk and treated to transient designers, bass enhancers and EQ to further boost the low end energy and remove problematic frequencies. The whole process took approximately 3 hours.

Reverb and Compression

Further Processing

To complete the kick and produce the sound I required, I compressed the kick heavily with a fast attack in order to level out the volume difference between the initial transient and the sub body that occurs directly afterwards. The kick was then treated to a small amount of reverb followed by a noise gate to remove any possible tail off.
