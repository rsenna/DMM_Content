Chapter 12 - Processors

Although compression is a requisite processor for the dance musician, further processors have an equal importance in sound design, production and mixing duties and therefore its important to understand how each processor can affect a timbre in different ways. Most processors produce obvious results but often the most misused is the limiter.

Limiting is an important effect in producing a sound that has both power and punch, however, excessive use of limiting will reduce the speaker excursion on a kick drum and result in a flat sounding mix.

CAUTION !

Be cautious of your volume monitoring levels in these examples, particularly in the heavy limiting. The volume is high and may damage speakers, headphones, or worse still your ears. Please exercise caution before listening to the limited versions.

No Limiting

No Limiting

In the above example the music has had no limiting applied. Although the mix has some dynamics, it is too quiet and will not hold up against other tracks played on the radio or club. Although loudness is unquantifiable, it is generally accepted that the louder something is, the better it is perceived to sound (within reason).

Average Limiting

A (subjective) Acceptable Amount of Limiting - watch your volume level before listening!

In the above example the music is played with a, subjectively, good average amount of limiting. This increases the overall volume of the mix helping it to match other tracks in terms of volume but the limiting has been carefully applied to also maintain some dynamics in the music.

Heavy Limiting

Excessive Limiting - watch your volume level before listening, this is loud!!

The above example has had excessive limiting applied. Note how when excessively limited, the music becomes much louder but equally looses its dynamic impact and almost sounds distorted. Finding the right medium between loudness and dynamics is a result of careful analysis of other tracks in the genre you're producing and experience.
