Chapter 25 - Recording Vocals

Note:

Due to copyright issues, the audio content described in the book for this particular chapter could not be used so we're using different vocals, provided by Amy.

Recording Vocals for electronic dance music doesn't require a professional studio or excessively priced equipment and it is perfectly possible to record them on a budget. All the examples on this page were recorded in a typical kitchen with a tiled floor (the best room we could find for maximum reverberations and sonic reflections). The vocals were recorded with an SE Z5600 valve microphone and Focusrite ISA One Pre-Amp. For sound protection, we employed home made Gobo's and the SE "reflexion" filter.

Note:

Please consider that these vocals are "as is" with absolutely no processing or "comping". Consequently, they may sound particularly "raw" since almost every vocalist is commonly treated to an absolute minimum of vocal "comping" (selecting the best parts of numerous vocal takes), auto-tuning, compression and reverb. I would like to thank Amy for her bravery in permitting me to use single vocal takes with no prior comping, correcting or processing whatsoever. Remember that the purpose of this exercise is not to concentrate on the performance but the sonic reflections of the room and how they're affected by the filters.

Bare Vocals

No Filters

The above example is a recording taken using nothing more than the SE Z5600 valve microphone in a cradle with a pop shield and a Focusrite ISA ONE pre-amplifier. Listen to the sonic reflections and reverberation returning to the microphone..

Reflexion Filter

Introducing a Reflexion Filter

For this next example we introduced the SE reflexion filter. Again, listen to how this filter reduces the sonic reflections and reverberations of the room. There are still reverberations present but they have been reduced.

Gobo's Only

Removing the reflexion filter and employing Gobos instead

In the above example, we removed the reflexion filter and relied entirely on the Gobo's. These have perhaps the most significant effect on the recording, massively reducing the sonic reverberation and reflections from the flat walls and tiled floor of the kitchen.

Reflexion Filter and Gobo's

Both Reflexion and Gobo's Together

In this final example, we employ both reflexion filter and Gobos. Combined these produce the most effective treatment resulting in a sonic character with very little of the rooms natural character, resulting in vocals that are easier to mix.
